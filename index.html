Previs Workflow for Gladiator

A step-by-step guide from storyboarding to final edit.

1. Storyboard

A series of drawings or images outlining the sequence of events. It provides a visual guide to help the team understand the story and plan the shot.

2. Mood Board

A collection of reference images, videos, or color swatches that capture the desired look and feel of the project, setting the visual tone.

3. Asset Collection and Generation

This phase involves gathering or creating all necessary 3D assets (modeling, texturing, rigging, etc.) for the shot, ensuring all elements are ready before animation begins.

4. Scene Blocking

Using simple proxies or rough shapes, scene blocking sets the composition, camera angles, and timing of actions. This is essential for testing the layout before detailed animation.

5. Environment Design

The environment is built using the generated assets, with initial lighting and mood settings that define the overall look of the shot.

6. Animation

This core stage involves animating the characters and scene elements. Several methods are available:

  • Manual 3D Animation: Hand‑animated frame by frame (₹600–₹1,000 per second).
  • Motion Capture: Using systems like Rokoko’s suite (~₹360,000 one‑time bundle cost).
  • AI Guided Animation: Options such as Move.ai (~₹129,000) or Wonder Studio (~₹12,300) for a 10‑minute video. Freemocap is open source and free.

7. Scene Preparation

The animation is integrated into the 3D environment with adjustments to the camera and lighting for improved composition and realism.

8. FX Addition

Special effects such as sparks, smoke, fire, etc. are added to create a dynamic and cinematic feel to the shot.

9. Final Lighting and Editing

Lighting and editing are fine-tuned to ensure every element aligns with the desired mood before final output.

10. Rendering

The fully assembled 3D scene is rendered into high-quality images or video, ready for review and further processing.

11. Final Compositing

Different layers—background, animated elements, and FX—are combined and color graded to produce a cohesive final output.

12. Final Edit

The shots are assembled in sequence and sound effects, music, and additional touches are added to complete the previs.

13. Conclusion

This workflow—from storyboarding to final edit—ensures a structured, efficient approach to creating a previs. Each stage builds on the previous one, streamlining planning and execution.
